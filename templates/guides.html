FIRE SURVIVAL GUIDE

CODE RED ACTIVE

Fire incidents spread rapidly. Immediate action and controlled evacuation procedures significantly increase survival rates.

Immediate Actions

  • Stay calm and identify the nearest emergency exit.
  • Do NOT use elevators during a fire.
  • Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation.
  • Cover nose and mouth using cloth if available.

If Trapped

  • Seal door gaps with wet cloth.
  • Signal rescuers through windows or lights.
  • Stay near floor level where air is cleaner.

FLOOD SURVIVAL GUIDE

SEVERE WATER LEVEL

Floodwaters can move rapidly and carry dangerous debris, electricity, and contamination.

Emergency Actions

  • Move immediately to higher ground.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwater.
  • Disconnect electricity if safe.
  • Carry emergency food and clean water.

After Flood

  • Do not drink contaminated water.
  • Avoid damaged buildings.
  • Monitor official emergency broadcasts.

EARTHQUAKE GUIDE

TECTONIC ALERT

DROP • COVER • HOLD

  • Drop to hands and knees immediately.
  • Take cover under sturdy furniture.
  • Hold onto shelter until shaking stops.

Safety Measures

  • Stay away from glass and heavy objects.
  • Do not run outside during shaking.
  • Expect aftershocks after major earthquakes.

CYCLONE PREPARATION

STORM WARNING

Before Cyclone

  • Store food, water, medicine, and flashlights.
  • Charge emergency communication devices.
  • Secure loose outdoor items.

During Cyclone

  • Stay indoors away from windows.
  • Follow evacuation instructions immediately.
  • Monitor emergency radio frequencies.

MEDICAL RESPONSE GUIDE

TRAUMA SUPPORT

CPR Protocol

  • Place hands at center of chest.
  • 100-120 compressions per minute.
  • Call emergency responders immediately.

Bleeding Control

  • Apply direct pressure using cloth.
  • Elevate injured area if possible.
  • Use tourniquet only for severe bleeding.
